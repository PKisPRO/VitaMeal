Dosa with Chutney

A crispy South Indian crepe served with flavorful coconut chutney

Prep Time

8 hours (including soaking)

Cook Time

20 minutes

Servings

4 people

About This Recipe

Dosa is a thin, crispy crepe made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, while the coconut chutney adds a perfect balance of flavors. This combination is not only delicious but also highly nutritious, providing a good source of carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats. The fermentation process makes it easier to digest and increases the bioavailability of nutrients.

Vegetarian Gluten-Free Fermented

Ingredients

For Dosa

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for grinding
  • Oil for cooking

For Coconut Chutney

  • 1 cup fresh coconut, grated
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1/4 cup roasted chana dal
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon urad dal
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for grinding

Instructions

Making Dosa Batter

  1. Wash and soak idli rice and urad dal separately for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind urad dal with fenugreek seeds to a smooth, fluffy batter.
  3. Grind rice to a slightly coarse batter.
  4. Mix both batters, add salt, and let it ferment overnight.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan and pour a ladle of batter in a circular motion.
  6. Drizzle oil around the edges and cook until crispy and golden brown.

Making Coconut Chutney

  1. Grind coconut, green chilies, ginger, and roasted chana dal to a smooth paste.
  2. Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency.
  3. Prepare tempering with oil, mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves.
  4. Add the tempering to the chutney and mix well.
  5. Serve with hot dosas.

Nutritional Information

Tips

  • Ensure proper fermentation of the batter for crispy dosas.
  • Use warm water while grinding the batter for better fermentation.
  • Don't overmix the batter after fermentation to maintain the airiness.
  • Keep the pan at medium heat for even cooking.
  • You can add various fillings like spiced potatoes for masala dosa.
